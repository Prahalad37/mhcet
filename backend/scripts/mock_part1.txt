PASSAGE (Q.89–Q.93): Read the following passage carefully and answer the questions.
The Indian judiciary has long grappled with the tension between judicial activism and judicial restraint. Proponents of
activism argue that courts must step in to protect constitutional rights when other branches of government fail to act.
Critics, however, contend that unelected judges should not override democratically elected legislatures. The Supreme
Court of India has expanded its jurisdiction significantly through Public Interest Litigation (PIL), allowing citizens to
approach it directly. While PIL has democratised access to justice, scholars warn that its overuse risks converting the
apex court into an ombudsman, diluting its role as a constitutional arbiter.
